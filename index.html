November 1, 2015
Elizabeth Lin

Montefiore Plaza in Hamilton Heights received an $18.4 million renovation in 2022 that gave it a larger footprint, new trees, and improved paving. But the most immediate comfort problems remain unsolved: there is almost no shade, seating is limited and fixed, and there is no infrastructure for community programming. This proposal adds the activation layer the renovation did not provide — shade, moveable seating, and a cultural and community identity. Follow this with four anchor statistics displayed visually: $18.4M renovation cost, 36 new trees (too young to provide shade), ~40,000 residents within 10-minute walk, and 0 programming spaces currently in place.

Proposed redesign introducing shade sails, flexible moveable seating, and native planting borders to support comfortable daily passive use without adding permanent programming infrastructure.
